Reactions

Terrorist attacks not only cause people to suffer physical injuries, but also have long-term psychological effects. These effects can include anxiety, depression, post-traumatic stress disorder and more. More than one-third of Americans say they are reluctant to engage in certain activities because of a terrorist incident. Terrorist attacks typically target certain locations and activities, such as busy public places or countries where global terrorist attacks are more frequent. Particularly in post-9/11 America, airplanes and skyscrapers are also seen as potential targets for terrorism According to a Gallup questionnaire in 2019, when people were asked: "As a result of the events relating to terrorism in recent years, would you say that now you are less willing to --, or not?", more than 40 percent said they would not want to travel abroad; about a third would not want to fly and attend crowded events; and a quarter would not want to enter a skyscraper.

The impact of terrorist attacks on people's physical and mental health and social stability is very serious. The government and law enforcement agencies need to take measures to prevent and deal with terrorist attacks.
However, to improve security, governments may resort to measures that infringe on privacy, such as public surveillance. Such measures may have a negative impact on personal freedom and the right to privacy, so there is a balance to be struck between security and personal freedom.
